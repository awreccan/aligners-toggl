Set up Siri — voice + reminders

This is a one-time setup on your iPhone. We’ll build two little shortcuts so you can just say “Hey Siri, Aligners off” when you eat and “Aligners on” when you’re done — hands-free, phone locked — and get lock-screen reminders to put them back in. Take it one numbered step at a time; nothing here is hard. Both halves matter — the voice logs your time, the reminders make sure they go back in.

First connect the app to your Toggl (tap the gear ⚙ on the main screen). Then come back here and the web links below will be filled in with your own token automatically.

0One-time: make the reminders list

Find the Reminders app on your phone (the white icon with coloured dots) and open it.

Tap Add List at the bottom right. Name it exactly Aligners (capital A, nothing else), pick any colour, and tap Done.

Why reminders and not just a phone alert? iPhone reminders ring on your lock screen even when your phone is on silent — that’s exactly what you want at the dinner table. That’s the whole trick.

1Build the “Aligners Off” shortcut

Open the Shortcuts app (comes with your iPhone). Tap the ＋ in the top-right corner to start a new one.

At the very top you’ll see a name like “New Shortcut” — tap it and rename it exactly Aligners Off. The exact name matters: it’s how Siri knows what to run when you speak.

Now add the actions, one at a time. Each time, tap ＋ Add Action, type the name in the search box, and tap it when it appears.

Action 1 — log that they came out. Add an action called Get Contents of URL. It’ll show a URL field and a Method. Set the Method to GET, then tap the URL field and paste this in (tap Copy — it already has your Toggl token baked in):

That’s the only backend action for this app — one web call does it. Nice and easy.

Action 2 — the 45-minute nudge (two actions together). First add Adjust Date. Tap the blue Date word in it and choose Current Date. Make sure it says Add (not Subtract), and set the amount to 45 Minutes.

Then add Add New Reminder right after it. Where it says “Reminder”, type Put your aligners back in 🦷. Tap Show More, set List to Aligners, and set the Alert (or Due Date) to the Adjusted Date chip — that blue chip is the result of the Adjust Date action you just added.

Action 3 — the 90-minute nudge. Do the exact same pair again: Adjust Date (Current Date, Add, 90 Minutes), then Add New Reminder with the text Aligners out 90 min — wrap it up 🦷, List Aligners, Alert = that new Adjusted Date.

+45 min “Put your aligners back in 🦷”
+90 min “Aligners out 90 min — wrap it up 🦷”

These nudges count from the moment you take your aligners out — think of them as a friendly “you’ve been out a while” tap on the shoulder, not a countdown. The real number — how much out-time you’ve got left today — always lives in the ring on the app’s main screen.

Action 2 — clear the nudges. Add Find Reminders. Tap Add Filter and set List is Aligners; tap Add Filter again and set Is Completed to off (the ones that haven’t fired yet).

Then add Remove Reminders. Its input should already be the reminders you just found — if not, tap its input and pick that. This wipes the pending nudges the moment your aligners are back in.

3Make Siri run them hands-free

Open each shortcut’s details (tap the ⓘ info button, or the “…” then settings) and turn OFF “Ask Before Running.” This is what lets Siri run them while your phone is locked, without you tapping anything.

That’s it. Test it: lock your phone and say “Hey Siri, Aligners off” — then later “Hey Siri, Aligners on.” 🎉
